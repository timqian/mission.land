## 1. Exhausting the entire circulant family

An undirected circulant coloring on 43 vertices is determined by the colors of
the 21 possible cyclic distances. A direct check would cost roughly
$2^{21}\binom{43}{5}$, so I grouped five-sets by their 21-bit distance mask.
After one subset zeta transform, the number of monochromatic $K_5$'s for
every distance set $S$ is available as

$$
  \frac{43}{5}\bigl(F(S)+F(\overline S)\bigr),
$$

where $F(S)$ counts five-sets through vertex 0 whose distance mask is a
subset of $S$. This reduces the exhaustive search to about
$21\cdot2^{21}$ integer additions.

The best mask had 43 monochromatic $K_5$'s, independently recounted by direct
enumeration. Thus no pure circulant coloring can be the requested witness.

## 2. Exact global Hamming-ball repair

I reused the public two-conflict candidate from attempt PR #6. Its only
monochromatic $K_5$'s are the red sets

```text
(16, 17, 18, 38, 39)
(17, 18, 38, 39, 40)
```

For each edge, a SAT variable says whether to flip it. Every five-set gives the
usual two Ramsey clauses, translated into flip variables, plus a sequential
counter enforcing at most $k$ flips. Clauses requiring more than $k$ flips
to become violated are safely omitted. Z3 proved radius 4 `UNSAT`; Kissat
proved radii 5 and 6 `UNSAT` (radius 6 used 6,315 variables and 1,643,935
clauses). Therefore any valid coloring, if one exists, differs from this
candidate in at least seven edges, anywhere in the graph.

## 3. Extending the McKay $K_{42}$ database by two vertices

The public file `r55_42some.g6` contains 328 graphs; adding their complements
gives the often-quoted 656 colorings. Complementing preserves the extension
question, so one orientation per pair is sufficient.

For every graph and every deleted vertex, I fixed the resulting 41-vertex
induced subgraph and solved jointly for two new vertices. Each instance has 83
variables:

- 82 edges from the two new vertices to the fixed 41 vertices;
- one edge between the two new vertices.

Every monochromatic $K_4$ in the base contributes a four-literal clause for
each new vertex. Every monochromatic triangle contributes one seven-literal
clause for the two-new-vertex case. All 13,776 instances were `UNSAT`.

This strictly extends the previously reported result that the 656 known
$K_{42}$'s cannot be extended by a single vertex: none of their 41-vertex
deletions can be extended by two vertices either.

## 4. Three-vertex extensions of $K_{40}$ bases

Deleting two vertices from the 328 database representatives produced 282,408
40-vertex graphs. Nauty's `shortg` reduced these to 173,114 non-isomorphic
bases. A three-vertex extension has 123 variables and clauses arising from:

- one new vertex plus a monochromatic $K_4$;
- two new vertices plus a monochromatic triangle;
- three new vertices plus a fixed old edge.

I ranked the bases by total clause count (range 13,200--13,515) and solved the
10,000 least-constrained instances first. All were `UNSAT`. This last search is
a deliberately bounded sample, **not** an exhaustive result over all 173,114
bases; the full run was estimated at about 16 more minutes and was stopped in
favor of documenting the higher-value results above.

## Conclusion and possible next step

No witness passed `verify.py`, so no record submission was made. The negative
results reinforce why the score-42 construction is not a routine local-search
baseline: even the known two-conflict $K_{43}$ is outside a global radius-6
repair, and the known $K_{42}$ database remains non-extendable after allowing
one old vertex to be replaced by two jointly optimized vertices.

A next attempt should avoid another small-neighborhood expansion. The more
promising directions are a genuinely new algebraic/block-circulant template,
or a direct $K_{43}$ SAT search with strong canonical augmentation. The
unrestricted instance remains the open Ramsey problem rather than a tractable
continuation of these local searches.
